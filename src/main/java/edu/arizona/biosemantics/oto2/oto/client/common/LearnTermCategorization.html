Introduction:

The following instructions are provided to help a human expert to categorize terms extracted from morphological descriptions by iPlant CharaParser:Learn or by ETC Semantic Markup tool. You will use a combination of your biological knowledge as well as linguistic knowledge to perform the categorization.

Category: Structures

General Guideline

  • Structure category includes named organs and organ parts .
  • Structures are entities that may have descriptors (i.e., characters). For example, 'apex of a leaf' is a structure because apex may have characters such as shape and color.
  • Structure category includes all nouns and pronouns.
Detailed Description with Examples


Terms/Words

Examples from text

Structure category holds terms that name organs or parts of organs in noun or adjective forms.

These also include terms that specify unnamed parts of organs, such as regions, areas, sides, borders, axis, commisure, suture, furrow, clump, bump, gap, aperture, slit, end, valleys, etc.

These also include abbreviated measurement labels, such HL [head length].

Named organs and organ parts:

  • Organ term in noun form: The anus is the opening of the rectum.
  • Organ term in adjective form: Dorsal and anal fins missing.
  • Part of organ: Apex acute. [apex is part of a leaf and its shape is acute]

Unnamed organ parts:

  • base oblique on inner side, rounded and prominent on outer side;
  • scales bicolored with dark central region.
  • False indusia inframarginal, strongly differentiated, 0.25--0.5 mm wide, forming pouch with constricted aperture on abaxial surface of ultimate segments.
  • Calyptra entire at base, often with a single slit extending toward apex.
  • ... extended along lateral-prefrontal suture

Abbreviated measurement labels:

  • HL 0.25cm ( HL stands for head length).

Terms which specify features of an organ in a noun form, such as zone, band, line etc.

Involucre lips with conspicuous dark marginal band 2--5 cells wide. [band is a feature and it is a noun]

Terms which specify life styles/habits.

Shrubs , subshrubs, or facultative annuals , glabrous or with scattered trichomes. [shrubs and annuals specify life styles/habits and they are nouns]



Category: Characters (including character states)

General Guidelines
  • Characters may be adjectives, or verbs that don't have an object.
  • Characters may be character state terms describing a structure, for example 'large'. The character state terms may have modifiers, such as frequency modifiers (e.g., rarely), but the modifiers should not be included in the Character category.
  • Characters may be characters such as 'coloration', 'shape', or 'size'.
  • Once identified a word as belong to Characters category, you need to categorize the word to their specific category, for example 'arrowlike' should be put in the 'shape' category. Another example, 'coloration' should be put int the 'character' category.
Detailed Description with Examples

Terms/Words

Examples from text

Terms that are adjectives and specify character states of a structure
  • ... [leaves] odor camphorlike ; [camphorlike describes the odor of leaves]
  • Microsporocarp masses entirely covered with arrowlike barbs. [arrowlike describes the shape of the barbs]

Verbs that describe character states of a structure and do not have an object. Verbs that take objects should be categorized into Other category
  • corolla tubes expanded to throats. [expanded describes the size of the corolla tubes]

Character terms
  • microsporophylls same size , auricles absent [size is a character/attribute/property of a structure]
  • Shell oval in profile. [profile is a character/attribute/property of a structure]

Category: Taxon Name

General Guidelines
  • Taxon names are either scientific names or components of them (e.g., specific epithets).
  • This does not include rank or status abbreviations (e.g., var., sp., subsp.).
  • If 'Taxon Name' category is not included, you can create a new category called 'taxon_name'.
Category: Other

General Guidelines
  • Terms that are not structures, descriptors, or taxon names. Such terms, standing alone, have little relevance to semantic markup of morphological descriptions.
  • Adverbs, verbs that take objects, prepositions, units of measurement or time, names of places or locations, environmental/ecological terms, many common English words, specialized notations, and certain terms produced by the software.
  • Terms belonging to 'Other' category should be left in the left column in the Categorization interface.
